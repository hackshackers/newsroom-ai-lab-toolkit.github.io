# 🖼️ Our Approach: Design Thinking for Newsrooms

### Starting a new reporting project doesn’t mean you already know the headline \- or even the angle. 

You might spot something intriguing, a gap, or a shift.

You get curious.

You talk to people.

You gather observations, facts, and perspectives before determining what the story really is.

Your job is to surface insights, not assume you already know the lede.

### This is the mindset at the core of **‘Design Thinking’** \-  

a methodology that begins with an **empathetic understanding of what’s happening for real people** (in this case, you, your colleagues and the communities you serve), setting aside assumptions about possible solutions. 

Just like in reporting, the Design Thinking process starting with clues, hunches, and observations to **get to the root of what people are experiencing currently, and then let the process expand our understanding and shape how a solution is built**.

## Design thinking is an ✨ iterative process ✨

### It helps us slow down enough to:

- **understand the experiences of real people,**   
- **challenge assumptions,**   
- **redefine problems given new informaion** and   
- **create innovative solutions which we can quickly prototype and test** 

The goal is to **identify alternative strategies and solutions that are not instantly apparent** with our initial level of understanding. 


### It guides us away from jumping to solutions 

Leaping into a particular solution, or starting with a tool looking for a problem it can solve, usually means limiting ourselves to current assumptions about the problem and what we already know how to do — which means we **miss out on real clarity that can lead to more effective and creative outcomes**.

In journalism terms, the Design Thinking process is similar to gathering possible reporting leads and tips – some might pan out, others won’t, but we don’t want to rule any out too early on in the process.

**Some of the most compelling stories come from reporting that leads us somewhere we couldn’t have predicted at the start**.


### The approach has been around for a while, just rarely inside newsrooms

🌱 **Roots in the 1960s–70s:** Design Thinking emerged as designers and engineers formalized creative problem-solving methods. Emphasis on rapid prototyping and testing through observation \- *design as a scientific approach to problem solving.*

🧪 **Practical evolution \- 2004 →  :** Refined by practitioners at Stanford’s d.school and IDEO, emphasizing empathy and experimentation.

🌍 **Broader adoption:** By the 1990s–2000s, it spread beyond design—into business, education, and social innovation—as a human-centered framework for tackling complex challenges.

(For more check out *[The History of Design Thinking](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/design-thinking-get-a-quick-overview-of-the-history?srsltid=AfmBOoqNucNf2PaqxkAFjRAwsFywEBDBvJcr6NQIpnWx61JJeigabdPl))*

Through our experiences working as the 'tech people' in newsrooms over the years we have found many parts of Design Thinking come in very useful when building solutions in the journalism world.
